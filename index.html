Welcome to Graze and Grow

Graze and Grow is dedicated to providing premium, organic horse manure compost for gardens and landscapes in the Woodinville area. Our mission is to help your soil and plants thrive through natural, sustainable practices—benefitting your garden, your community, and the planet.

The Benefits of Organic Compost

Using organic compost in your garden:

  • Improves soil fertility and nutrient content
  • Increases water retention and reduces irrigation needs
  • Boosts plant health, yield, and resilience
  • Reduces dependence on chemical fertilizers
  • Supports beneficial soil microbes and earthworms
  • Reduces landfill waste and greenhouse gas emissions

Compost is a natural way to enhance your garden’s productivity and support a healthy ecosystem.

How We Make Our Compost

Our compost is produced right here in Woodinville using only clean horse manure and bedding from healthy, well-cared-for horses. We:

  • Source manure free from persistent herbicides
  • Turn and aerate the pile regularly for optimal decomposition
  • Allow compost to age and mature for rich, dark organic matter
  • Screen the finished compost for a fine, even texture
  • Test batches to ensure quality and safety for your plants

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Is your compost safe for vegetable gardens? Yes! Our compost is herbicide-free and safe for all edible and ornamental plants.
  • How much compost do I need? Most gardens benefit from 1–2 inches of compost mixed into the top 6 inches of soil every year.
  • Does compost smell? Properly aged compost smells earthy, not unpleasant. Fresh manure is never sold as finished product.
  • How should I apply compost? Spread an even layer on beds or mix into soil before planting. See our usage tips below.

Tips for Using Compost

  • Apply 1–2 inches annually to garden beds for best results.
  • Mulch around trees and shrubs to conserve moisture and suppress weeds.
  • Topdress lawns with a thin layer for healthier grass.
  • Store compost in a dry, covered spot until use.
